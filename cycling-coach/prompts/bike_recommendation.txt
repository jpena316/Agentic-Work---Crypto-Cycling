RANKING INSTRUCTIONS

Rank all bikes from best to worst fit for this rider. Consider these factors in your analysis:

1. Terrain fit: Match the bike's intended terrain against the rider's dominant terrain. An endurance bike is a poor match for a flat-roads criterium rider; an aero bike is a poor match for a climbing-focused rider.

2. Riding style: Match the bike's geometry and character to the rider's style. A grand fondo rider needs compliance and comfort for long efforts; a frequent short-ride rider may prioritize stiffness and responsiveness.

3. Fitness level and goals: A recreational rider does not need the most aggressive race geometry. An advanced rider pursuing racing performance is poorly served by a relaxed endurance bike.

4. Geometry fit: Consider stack/reach ratios. A higher stack suits endurance riders; a lower stack suits aggressive racers. Confirm the bike's geometry category is appropriate for the rider's goals.

5. Component value: Given the rider's training focus and goals, assess whether the groupset and components are well-matched to their use case.
